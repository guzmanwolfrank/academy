What exactly are futures contracts and how do they differ from stocks?
A futures contract is an agreement to buy or sell a specific asset at a predetermined price on a future date. Unlike stocks where you own a piece of a company, futures contracts are obligations to trade commodities, financial instruments, or other assets at a future date. They're primarily used for hedging risk or speculating on price movements.

Should I Trade My Own Capital or Use an Online Prop Firm?
What's an Online Prop Firm?

They provide traders access to larger capital accounts after passing trading challenges. Pay a fee, pass their test, trade their money for a profit split (typically 70-90%).

Prop Firm Pros:

Lower Starting Capital ($200-1000 vs. $25k+)
Access to Bigger Trading Accounts
Limited Downside Risk
Faster Path to Full-time Trading

Prop Firm Cons:

Share Profits with Firm
Trading Restrictions/Rules
Monthly/Challenge Fees
More Psychological Pressure

Trading Your Own Money:

Keep 100% of Profits
Trade Without Restrictions
No Extra Fees
Faster profit withdrawal from your retail broker. No payout denials.

Bottom Line:

Smart traders often do both: Start with prop firms to learn discipline and trade bigger size, then gradually add personal capital trading as you build wealth.
